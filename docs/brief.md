## What is this site?

The new marketing website for **Design Space Sdn Bhd** — and the public face of a **simultaneous strategic rebrand**. The agency is repositioning from "Apps, Branding & Marketing Company" (the 2014–2022 framing) to **"AI Product, Data & Automation Studio"** *(✅ confirmed 2026-05-16)*. The new site is the launch vehicle for this pivot.

Internal project codename: **DS Rebirth** (confirmed 2026-05-16).

## The new strategic direction (confirmed 2026-05-16)

**Positioning:**
> Design Space is an AI Product, Data & Automation Studio that helps businesses launch, automate, and scale through branding, AI-ready websites, custom apps, data intelligence, and IoT-connected systems.

**Core framework — The Design Space Digital Loop:**
> Brand → Website → Leads → CRM → Automation → App → IoT → Data → AI → Growth

**Strategic intent:** use the website as the entry point — attract quality clients, diagnose their business operations, then upsell automation / custom systems / IoT / data / AI work. Build long-term client relationships and recurring revenue, not one-off design projects.

Full direction: [`docs/assets/design-space-rebranding-starter-kit.md`](assets/design-space-rebranding-starter-kit.md). Everything below is the condensed working version.

## Why rebuild?

Two converging reasons:

1. **No public portfolio.** ~12 years of work, ~8-person team, strong client roster — but designspace.my is just a placeholder. Nothing online supports the company's positioning.
2. **Active repositioning.** The agency is pivoting from a creative/digital studio model into an AI / data / automation studio model. The new website is the launch surface for that repositioning — not just a brochure refresh.

Each pillar maps to a productized package (Launch · AI-Ready Website · Automate · Build · Connect · Intelligence · AI Lab) with pricing bands RM3K → RM300K+, plus monthly retainers RM300–RM20K/month. Details in rebrand doc §10 + §18.

## Target audience

**Best-fit customers:** SMEs with 10–100 staff · multi-outlet · offline/physical operations · heavy WhatsApp users · Excel-driven · manual customer follow-up · businesses with machines/sensors/equipment · wants an app but unsure how to start · wants AI but no data foundation · revenue but weak digital structure.

**Best-fit industries:** Laundry / vending / self-service · Marine / logistics / offshore · Property / short stay · Retail chains · F&B chains · Education / training · Equipment rental · Service companies · Manufacturing / workshop · Medical / wellness · Automotive · Construction / renovation.

**Customers to filter out:** price-only comparators · "RM800 website" hunters · no intention to improve operations · won't provide content · reject monthly maintenance · expect unlimited revisions · want advanced AI with no budget or data · no decision-maker involved.

## Visual identity (confirmed 2026-05-16)

**Decision:** the established **maroon + white** brand identity carries forward into the new site. The premium-tech palette proposed in the rebrand doc is *not* being adopted — the brand is already established and Rick wants continuity.

### Canonical brand (carried forward)
- **Primary color:** maroon — the established Design Space brand color (also the agency's office wall paint). **Open: exact hex / PMS value still needed.**
- **Mark color:** white
- **Logo:** `designspace` wordmark — light "design" + bold "space" — with the rocket-and-star icon above
- **Descriptor on the lockup:** ✅ **Dropped** (2026-05-16) — the lockup is just wordmark + rocket. The new positioning lives in the website hero and other touchpoints, not on the logo.

### Web aesthetic (confirmed 2026-05-16 — Tessie-inspired dark theme)

The site adopts a **dark, ultra-clean, premium aesthetic in the style of [tessie.com](https://www.tessie.com)** — near-black backgrounds (`#0A0A0B`), crisp near-white text (`#F5F5F7`), muted grey body (`#9A9AA0`), and **maroon as the singular vivid accent color** (CTAs, key numbers, hover states, hero ambient glow). Generous whitespace, modern geometric sans-serif type, restrained motion (fade-up + count-ups).

Full specs (colors, typography, spacing, motion, components, hero patterns) live in [`docs/design-system.md`](design-system.md). Reference: [tessie.com](https://www.tessie.com) + the [adapted prompt at `docs/assets/tessie-inspired-website-prompt.md`](assets/tessie-inspired-website-prompt.md).

**Synthesis with the earlier "maroon + white" decision:** maroon remains the canonical brand color and continues to define the agency. The Tessie framework uses ONE vivid accent, and **maroon takes that slot**. So both decisions hold — maroon continues; the surface around it is dark + modern instead of maroon-dominant.

### 2022 yellow-black collateral system (confirmed 2026-05-16)
Stays as a sanctioned **collateral-only** system — used for decks, posters, and print materials. **Not on the website.** The website remains clean maroon + white.
